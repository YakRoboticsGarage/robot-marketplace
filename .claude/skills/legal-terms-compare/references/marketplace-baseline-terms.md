# Marketplace Baseline Terms (ConsensusDocs 751 Framework)

Default terms the marketplace generates when auto-creating subcontracts. Used as the comparison baseline when only one document is provided.

## The 12 Dimensions — Marketplace Defaults

| # | Dimension | Marketplace Default | Rationale |
|---|-----------|-------------------|-----------|
| 1 | Indemnification | Mutual, proportional fault | Balanced; compliant with all state anti-indemnity statutes |
| 2 | Limitation of liability | 1x total task fee | Industry standard for professional services |
| 3 | Insurance | CGL $1M/$2M, E&O $1M/$2M, Aviation $2M (if aerial) | Meets most DOT minimums |
| 4 | Payment terms | Milestone billing, net-30 from delivery acceptance | Faster than industry norm (net-60/90) |
| 5 | Retainage | 10% until acceptance; reduced to 0% after 50% completion (MI public) | Per Michigan statute |
| 6 | Data ownership | Client owns all deliverables upon payment | Clear and simple |
| 7 | Standard of care | Reasonably prudent surveyor | Professional standard, not warranty |
| 8 | Consequential damages | Mutually waived | Standard for professional services |
| 9 | Dispute resolution | Negotiation (30 days) → Mediation → Binding arbitration | Keeps disputes out of court |
| 10 | Termination | For cause (material breach + 10-day cure) + for convenience (pay for work completed) | Balanced |
| 11 | Change orders | Written approval required, priced at contract rates + 15% markup | Protects both parties |
| 12 | PLS responsibility | Operator's PLS stamps deliverables | Standard for survey subcontracts |

## Why ConsensusDocs 751

ConsensusDocs 751 (Subcontract Agreement for Design-Build) is chosen over AIA A401 because:
- More balanced between contractor and subcontractor (AIA A401 favors owners)
- Proportional fault indemnification by default
- Explicit financing information rights for subcontractors
- Developed by a coalition including AGC, not just architects
