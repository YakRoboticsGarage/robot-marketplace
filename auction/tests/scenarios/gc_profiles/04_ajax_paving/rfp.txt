Project: I-96 Rehabilitation — Mill & Overlay with Drainage Improvements

Length: 3.8 miles (both directions)

BACKGROUND:

Ajax Paving Industries has been awarded the MDOT contract for rehabilitation
of the I-96 corridor in western Oakland County. The project involves milling
and resurfacing of all travel lanes, shoulder reconstruction, drainage structure
replacement, and ADA-compliant sidewalk ramp upgrades at interchange areas.

Pre-construction survey data is required to support design and utility
coordination. This RFP seeks proposals for two distinct survey tasks that
may be awarded separately to different firms.

SCOPE OF WORK:

TASK A: TOPOGRAPHIC SURVEY
- Full corridor topographic survey, 50-foot offsets each side of pavement edge
- Aerial LiDAR acquisition at USGS Quality Level 2 minimum (2 pts/m²)
- RTK-GPS ground control network with minimum 15 control points
- Cross-sections at 25-foot intervals along centerline
- Map all drainage structures (catch basins, manholes, culverts, outlets)
- Map all above-ground utilities (poles, cabinets, vaults, hydrants)
- Map all signs, signals, barriers, guardrail termini
- Pavement condition documentation (visible distress, patching, joint condition)
- Accuracy: +/-0.05 ft horizontal, +/-0.05 ft vertical (NSSDA 95%)
- Coordinate system: Michigan State Plane South Zone, NAD83(2011), NAVD88 (GEOID18)
- Deliverables:
  * LandXML surface model
  * DXF planimetric map (MDOT CAD standard layers)
  * LAS point cloud (ASPRS classified, Class 2/6/9 minimum)
  * GeoTIFF orthomosaic (2-inch GSD minimum)
  * Cross-section sheets (PDF) at 25-ft stations
  * FGDC-compliant metadata

TASK B: SUBSURFACE UTILITY DETECTION
- Ground-penetrating radar (GPR) survey of full corridor
- Survey limits: pavement edge to 25-foot offset each side
- Locate all underground utilities to Quality Level B per ASCE 38-22
- Identify utility type, approximate depth, and horizontal position
- Target utilities: water, sanitary, storm, gas, electric, telecom, fiber
- Special attention to drainage crossings at all interchange ramp areas
- Equipment: 400 MHz + 900 MHz antenna array minimum
- Accuracy: +/-0.5 ft horizontal, +/-1.0 ft vertical for depth estimates
- Coordinate system: Michigan State Plane South Zone, NAD83(2011)
- Deliverables:
  * DXF utility map with ASCE 38 quality level designations
  * PDF summary report with utility conflict matrix
  * CSV coordinate file of all identified utility crossings
  * Raw GPR data files for independent review

REQUIREMENTS:
- Task A: Michigan Professional Land Surveyor (PLS) supervision required
- Task A: FAA Part 107 remote pilot certification for aerial operations
- Task A: Minimum $2M professional liability insurance
- Task B: GPR operator with minimum 5 years utility detection experience
- Both Tasks: MDOT Section 104.09 compliance (survey on state trunklines)
- Both Tasks: MOT (Maintenance of Traffic) plan required
- Provide proof of insurance per Ajax standard requirements
