ANLAAN CORPORATION / C.A. HULL COMPANY JOINT VENTURE
DESIGN-BUILD SUBCONTRACT TERMS — PROFESSIONAL SURVEY SERVICES

Contract: IM-94-23456
Form: ANL-DB-SURV-2026-01

These terms govern survey subcontracts issued under the I-94 Modernization
Design-Build Contract. They incorporate design-build-specific provisions
that differ from standard hard-bid survey subcontracts.

1. SCOPE AND COMPENSATION
   1.1. Subcontractor provides professional survey services as specified in
        individual Task Authorizations issued under this agreement.
   1.2. Compensation: lump sum per task authorization, with unit-rate
        provisions for changed conditions or additional scope.
   1.3. Payment terms: Net 30 from approved invoice.
   1.4. Retainage: 10% withheld until final deliverable acceptance and
        MDOT review completion.
        NOTE: Design-build retainage is higher than standard (5%) due to
        iterative design review process.

2. DESIGN-BUILD INTEGRATION
   2.1. Survey data feeds directly into design development — accuracy and
        timeliness are critical path items.
   2.2. Subcontractor shall participate in design coordination meetings
        as requested (typically biweekly).
   2.3. Subcontractor may be asked to perform supplemental survey based
        on evolving design requirements. Supplemental work authorized
        via written Task Authorization amendment only.
   2.4. Subcontractor shall coordinate with other design-build team members
        including geotechnical, structural, and environmental subconsultants.
   2.5. MDOT has direct review and comment authority on all survey
        deliverables, even though contract is with the JV.

3. INSURANCE REQUIREMENTS
   3.1. Commercial General Liability: $2,000,000 per occurrence / $4,000,000 aggregate
   3.2. Professional Liability (E&O): $2,000,000 per claim / $4,000,000 aggregate
   3.3. Automobile Liability: $1,000,000 combined single limit
   3.4. Workers' Compensation: Statutory limits with USL&H if applicable
   3.5. Unmanned Aircraft (if applicable): $5,000,000 per occurrence
   3.6. Umbrella/Excess: $5,000,000
   3.7. Both Anlaan Corporation and C.A. Hull Company must be named
        as Additional Insureds on CGL and umbrella policies.
   3.8. MDOT must be named as Additional Insured on CGL policy.

4. PROFESSIONAL STANDARDS
   4.1. All survey work performed under direct supervision of Michigan PLS.
   4.2. PLS shall stamp and certify all survey deliverables.
   4.3. Comply with MDOT Survey and Mapping Standards (current edition).
   4.4. Comply with ASPRS positional accuracy standards for point cloud data.
   4.5. For tunnel work: comply with OSHA 29 CFR 1926.1200 (Confined Spaces
        in Construction) and MIOSHA Part 90 (Confined Space Entry).

5. SAFETY — CONFINED SPACE OPERATIONS
   5.1. Subcontractor shall provide a project-specific Confined Space
        Entry Program for MDOT and JV review prior to tunnel entry.
   5.2. All tunnel entry personnel must complete:
        - 40-hour HAZWOPER training (or 8-hour refresher if current)
        - Confined space entry training per OSHA 29 CFR 1926.1200
        - CPR/First Aid certification
   5.3. Atmospheric monitoring required continuously during tunnel operations.
   5.4. Rescue plan: Subcontractor shall provide either a trained rescue
        team or mechanical retrieval system approved by the JV safety manager.
   5.5. Daily safety briefings required before each tunnel entry.

6. SCHEDULE
   6.1. Time is of the essence. Design-build schedule is contractually
        binding with MDOT.
   6.2. Subcontractor shall mobilize within 3 calendar days of NTP.
   6.3. Liquidated damages: $1,000/calendar day for late completion.
        NOTE: Higher than standard due to design-build schedule criticality.
   6.4. Subcontractor shall provide weekly schedule updates to JV
        project manager.

7. DELIVERABLE REVIEW
   7.1. JV reviews deliverables within 7 business days.
   7.2. MDOT has additional 10 business days for independent review.
   7.3. Total review cycle: up to 17 business days (longer than standard).
   7.4. Subcontractor shall address JV and MDOT comments within 5
        business days of receipt.

8. QUALITY MANAGEMENT
   8.1. Subcontractor shall maintain a QA/QC plan on file with the JV.
   8.2. Independent QC checkpoints required per MDOT standards.
   8.3. JV reserves the right to perform independent accuracy verification.

9. INDEMNIFICATION
   9.1. Subcontractor shall defend, indemnify, and hold harmless the JV,
        its member companies, MDOT, and the State of Michigan from any
        claims arising from Subcontractor's negligent acts or omissions.
   9.2. Design-build liability: Subcontractor acknowledges that survey
        errors may have downstream design and construction impacts and
        accepts professional responsibility accordingly.

10. OWNERSHIP AND LICENSING
    10.1. All deliverables become property of MDOT upon acceptance.
    10.2. JV and Subcontractor retain right to use data for project
          administration and professional portfolio.
    10.3. Subcontractor grants perpetual license to JV and MDOT for
          all data, models, and derivative products.

11. DISPUTE RESOLUTION
    11.1. Step 1: Direct negotiation between project managers (14 days).
    11.2. Step 2: Escalation to JV principals and Subcontractor principal (14 days).
    11.3. Step 3: Mediation under AAA Construction Industry rules.
    11.4. Step 4: Binding arbitration under AAA rules.
    11.5. Venue: Wayne County, Michigan.

12. TERMINATION
    12.1. JV may terminate for convenience with 14 days written notice.
    12.2. JV may terminate for cause with 7 days written notice and
          opportunity to cure.
    12.3. Subcontractor compensated for accepted work through termination date.
    12.4. If MDOT terminates the design-build contract, JV may terminate
          all subcontracts immediately.

13. FEDERAL REQUIREMENTS (DESIGN-BUILD SPECIFIC)
    13.1. This project uses federal-aid highway funds.
    13.2. DBE participation goal: 15% of survey subcontract value.
    13.3. Buy America provisions apply to permanent materials.
    13.4. Davis-Bacon wage rates apply to field survey technicians.
    13.5. E-Verify compliance required.
    13.6. FHWA has oversight authority on design-build projects.
