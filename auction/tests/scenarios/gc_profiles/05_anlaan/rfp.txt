Project: I-94 Modernization — Drainage Tunnel Rehabilitation

Design-Build Contractor: Anlaan Corporation / C.A. Hull Company JV

BACKGROUND:

The Michigan Department of Transportation is modernizing the I-94 corridor
in east Detroit as part of the I-94 Modernization Program. This phase
addresses rehabilitation of the aging drainage tunnel system beneath the
freeway, originally constructed in the 1940s-1950s. The tunnel system
collects stormwater from the depressed freeway section and conveys it to
the Detroit Water and Sewerage Department combined sewer system.

The design-build team (Anlaan/C.A. Hull JV) requires comprehensive survey
services to support design development, tunnel condition assessment, and
construction monitoring. Survey services will be contracted through the
design-build team with MDOT oversight.

SCOPE OF WORK:

TASK 1: PRE-CONSTRUCTION TOPOGRAPHIC SURVEY
- Aerial LiDAR corridor survey of I-94 mainline and all interchange ramps
- Corridor width: 200 feet (100 ft each side of centerline)
- Approximate survey area: 45 acres over 3.2 miles
- RTK-GPS ground control network: minimum 20 control points
- Cross-sections at 50-foot intervals along mainline centerline
- Cross-sections at 25-foot intervals through interchange areas
- Map all surface features:
  * Pavement edges, lane lines, shoulders
  * Drainage inlets, manholes, catch basins, outfall structures
  * Overhead and underground utility indicators
  * Signs, signals, lighting, ITS devices
  * Barriers, guardrail, bridge rails, retaining walls
  * Sidewalks, curbs, ADA ramps
- Accuracy: +/-0.05 ft horizontal and vertical (NCHRP 748 Category 1A)
- Coordinate system: Michigan State Plane South Zone, NAD83(2011), NAVD88 (GEOID18)
- Deliverables:
  * LandXML surface model
  * DXF planimetric map (MDOT CAD standard layer naming)
  * LAS point cloud (ASPRS classified, Class 2 minimum)
  * GeoTIFF orthomosaic (2-inch GSD minimum)
  * Cross-section sheets (PDF and DXF)
  * Ground control report with network adjustment
  * FGDC-compliant metadata for all datasets

TASK 2: TUNNEL 3D SCANNING AND AS-BUILT SURVEY
- Terrestrial LiDAR scanning of the complete drainage tunnel system
- Approximately 6,000 linear feet of tunnel (multiple branches)
- Tunnel dimensions vary: 4 ft to 12 ft diameter
- Point spacing: not greater than 0.05 ft on all structural elements
- Document and measure:
  * Wall thickness variations (where accessible)
  * Joint condition and separation
  * Invert profile and grade
  * Lateral connections and junction chambers
  * Visible deterioration (spalling, cracking, corrosion, root intrusion)
  * Sediment depth at 25-ft intervals
  * Cross-sectional geometry at 25-ft intervals
- Confined space entry required per OSHA 29 CFR 1926.1200
  * Permit-required confined space program
  * Atmospheric monitoring (O2, H2S, CO, LEL)
  * Rescue team or retrieval system
  * Entry supervisor, attendant, and authorized entrants
- Coordinate system: tied to Task 1 control network
- Deliverables:
  * e57 registered point cloud (full tunnel system)
  * LAS classified point cloud
  * DXF cross-sections at 25-ft intervals
  * Tunnel condition report with photo documentation
  * Invert profile (LandXML)
  * Wall thickness measurement log
  * Confined space entry logs and atmospheric monitoring records

TASK 3: CONTINUOUS TBM ALIGNMENT MONITORING
- Real-time alignment monitoring during tunnel boring machine operations
- Robotic total station with automated target tracking
- Continuous position reporting to TBM guidance system
- Requires on-site tunnel survey manager with minimum 10 years
  tunnel survey experience
- 24/7 coverage during active boring operations (estimated 60 days)
- NOT ELIGIBLE FOR AUTOMATED ROBOT BIDDING — this task requires a
  specialized human vendor with tunnel construction survey experience.
  Include this task in SOQ for informational purposes only.
