# Feedback: Legal Framework for Robot Survey Contracts

**Date:** 2026-03-28
**Source:** Founder
**Context:** Robots on the marketplace have owners and operators. When a GC hires a robot for a $72K survey task, there are legal agreements involved — insurance, liability, licensing, indemnification.

---

## Questions to Answer

1. What legal agreements exist between a survey firm and a GC today?
   - Master Service Agreement (MSA)
   - Subcontractor Agreement
   - Insurance certificates (COI)
   - Indemnification clauses
   - Limitation of liability

2. What certifications/licenses must a survey operator have?
   - State-licensed Professional Surveyor
   - FAA Part 107 (drone operators)
   - OSHA certifications
   - Professional liability (E&O) insurance

3. What liability does the robot operator carry?
   - If the survey data is wrong and the GC builds based on it?
   - If the robot damages property during the survey?
   - If the robot injures someone on-site?

4. What should be visible on a robot profile?
   - Operator's legal entity name
   - License numbers (surveyor, FAA)
   - Insurance certificates (GL, professional liability, auto)
   - Standard terms of service
   - Limitation of liability amount

5. What should happen at auction award?
   - Auto-generated subcontractor agreement?
   - Insurance certificate exchange?
   - Scope of work confirmation?
   - Legal review period before work starts?

## Where This Shows in the Demo

- **Robot profile:** Legal section with certs, insurance, terms
- **Auction award screen:** Agreement summary before work begins
- **Result/receipt:** Reference to executed agreement
