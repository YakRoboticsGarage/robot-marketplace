# Senior PM Feedback: Market Focus & Use Cases

**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Source:** Senior product manager review
**Severity:** Strategic — blocks product-market fit

---

## Raw Feedback

- "It's missing actual use-cases"
- "I don't know what to do with this"
- "What would a robot be actually hired for?"
- "Target, narrow your market"

## Interpretation

The current product presents as a horizontal platform ("hire any robot for any task") without a clear wedge industry. Enterprise buyers don't search for "hire a robot" — they search for solutions to specific problems in their domain. The demo shows temperature readings, but no buyer wakes up thinking "I need a robot to read a temperature sensor." They think "I need to pass my HVAC compliance inspection."

## Key Questions to Answer

1. **Which specific industry should we target first?** Construction, insurance, safety/compliance, agriculture, logistics, energy, real estate?

2. **What are the actual jobs-to-be-done?** Not "sensor reading" but "pre-bid site survey for a highway construction contract" or "quarterly fire safety inspection for insurance renewal."

3. **Which robots and sensors map to real purchasing decisions?** What does a civil engineer actually need measured? What does an insurance adjuster need photographed?

4. **Why a marketplace vs. direct partnership?** If I'm a construction firm, why wouldn't I just buy robots or contract with a single robotics company? What makes an auction/marketplace model superior?

5. **Why agent-mediated?** What's the advantage of having an AI agent manage the robot hiring vs. a human clicking through a portal?

## North Star Constraint

The long-term vision is robots on the Moon. The first industry should be chosen such that it naturally extends to lunar operations — construction and site surveying are the most obvious candidates (Artemis program = lunar construction).

## Founder's Example

"Hiring a robot to do spec confirmation for crafting and submitting a bid to construct a highway" — the civil engineer needs site data (soil density, grade measurements, existing infrastructure survey) to prepare an accurate bid. Currently done by sending a survey crew (expensive, slow). A robot marketplace could provide this faster and cheaper, with the data structured for direct inclusion in bid documents.

## What Research Must Prove

1. The chosen wedge has real purchasing budget (not hypothetical demand)
2. A marketplace is better than direct partnership (multi-vendor, on-demand, competition drives quality/price)
3. Agent mediation adds value (structured data, automated workflows, recurring tasks)
4. The path from Earth wedge → lunar operations is credible
