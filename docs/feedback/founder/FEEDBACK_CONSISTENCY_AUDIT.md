# Founder Feedback: Visual Consistency Audit Needed

**Date:** 2026-03-27
**Source:** Direct founder input after construction aesthetics overhaul

---

## Raw Feedback

- "The direction is correct, but the audit has not led to a consistent feel across the board"
- "There are a mix of fonts that aren't in harmony"
- "Same as the copy"
- "'Search' doesn't feel right for the user action"
- "The blue band at the top also doesn't quite make sense"

## Interpretation

The construction aesthetics overhaul applied the right individual changes (squared corners, navy+orange palette, IBM Plex Sans headers) but they haven't been harmonized into a cohesive system. The result feels like a collection of correct design tokens rather than a unified design.

Specific issues to investigate:
1. **Font mixing** — IBM Plex Sans + Inter + JetBrains Mono may be too many families. Where does each appear? Are the weights and sizes creating a clear hierarchy or visual noise?
2. **"Search" button text** — too generic for the construction context. "Upload RFP spec or describe your survey" placeholder sets a specific expectation; the action button should match (e.g., "Get Quotes" or just an arrow icon)
3. **Navy header bar** — may feel disconnected from the white page below. Procore/Trimble have more visual continuity between their nav and content area. The contrast jump from navy to white is stark.
4. **Copy tone inconsistency** — some text feels construction-professional, some still feels generic SaaS. Need a full pass.
