# Pitch Critique: VP of Preconstruction, $200M Michigan Highway GC

**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Reviewer persona:** VP of Preconstruction, mid-market Michigan highway GC ($180-220M annual revenue, 12-18 active MDOT projects)

---

## Does this solve a real problem?

Partially. The pain is real: during peak letting season (March-May), I absolutely lose bids because survey turnaround kills my schedule. But the deck overstates it. I don't wait "2-3 weeks" for survey data -- I wait 5-10 business days from a firm I already trust, and I'm usually planning around that lead time months in advance. The crisis isn't the normal cycle; it's the emergency re-survey when we win a project and field conditions don't match plan sheets. That's where speed has real dollar value. The deck should lead with the emergency use case, not the routine one.

The "60-80% cost reduction" claim is a red flag. Survey is 0.3-0.5% of my project cost. I don't choose a survey provider to save $4K; I choose one whose data doesn't create a $400K earthwork quantity error. If you lead with cost savings, you sound like you don't understand my risk profile.

## What claims don't ring true?

- **"Certified bids in minutes."** I don't want speed on the bid -- I want speed on the deliverable. Getting three quotes fast is nice but not my bottleneck. Getting a usable surface model in 48 hours instead of 10 days -- that's the pitch.
- **"0/15 have in-house drone survey."** This is misleading. We don't have in-house drone programs because we outsource to PLS-stamped firms with E&O insurance we can flow claims through. That's a feature, not a gap. Your platform needs to address professional liability, not ignore it.
- **"PLS-affiliated drone operators."** This phrase is meaningless. Either the deliverable carries a PLS stamp or it doesn't. "Affiliated" is not a legal term and would make my project manager nervous.
- **"Same sensors, same workflows... lunar."** The moment you mention the moon in a construction pitch, I stop taking you seriously. It signals you're building a tech company that uses my industry as a stepping stone, not a company that solves my problems.

## What's missing that I'd need before a pilot?

1. **Professional liability chain.** If your operator's surface model is wrong and I underbid earthwork by 30,000 CY, who carries that? Show me the E&O policy structure and how claims flow.
2. **Deliverable specs in my language.** I need LandXML or .TIN for HeavyBid, DWG with Civil 3D feature codes matching MDOT standards (not generic DXF). Show me you know the difference between a DTM and a DSM and why it matters for quantity takeoff.
3. **MDOT compliance specifics.** Which MDOT survey standards does this meet? Reference the Geodetic Survey Section requirements. "Full compliance trail" means nothing without naming the standard.
4. **A named PLS on the deliverable.** Not "PLS-affiliated." An actual Michigan-licensed PLS who stamps the final product and carries E&O.
5. **One real project example.** Even a pro-bono survey on a real MDOT corridor with real deliverables imported into HeavyBid. I need to see the output, not the auction engine.

## What would make me do a pilot?

Give me a free survey on one of my upcoming projects -- a straightforward 10-acre topo for a resurfacing bid. Deliver LandXML that imports clean into HeavyBid. Have a Michigan PLS stamp it. If the surface matches our traditional survey within 0.1 ft vertical, I'll pay for the next one. Don't pitch me the platform; pitch me the deliverable.

## Jargon that's wrong or off-putting

- **"MCP protocol," "ERC-8004," "x402," "USDC on Base"** -- None of this means anything to me. I pay with a purchase order or a credit card. Blockchain is a negative signal in construction; it says "tech solution looking for a problem."
- **"AI agents post tasks"** -- I post tasks. My estimator posts tasks. We're not "AI agents." If you mean your software helps me write a scope, say that.
- **"Agentic procurement"** -- This will get your email deleted unread.
- **"GMV"** -- Investor jargon in a customer-facing context.
- **"Robot task auction"** -- I'm hiring a survey crew, not buying a robot. "Survey marketplace" would land; "robot task auction" sounds like science fiction.

## Bottom line

You've done real research -- the 43 MDOT RFPs, the GC identification, the HeavyBid/B2W references show homework. But the deck is written for investors, not for me. If you want me as your pilot customer, show up with a deliverable, a PLS, and an E&O policy -- not an auction engine and a blockchain rail.
