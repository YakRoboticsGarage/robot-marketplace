## Detailed Entries

### 1. I-94 Modernization Detroit (Package 1: Burns Ave - Barrett Ave)

**Agency:** MDOT
**Type:** Highway reconstruction, interchange modernization, drainage
**URL:** https://www.michigan.gov/mdot/projects-studies/i-94-modernization-project-detroit
**Value:** $2B+ total program; construction anticipated 2028-2031
**Date:** RFP for tunnel design-build expected February 2026

**Project Scope:**
Rebuild 7 miles of interstate from east of Conner Avenue to east of I-96 interchange in Detroit. Includes modernizing interchanges at Conner Avenue, Gratiot Avenue and M-10 (Lodge Freeway), replacing more than 70 bridges, lengthening entrance and exit ramps.

**Survey Requirements:**
- Full design survey per MDOT Chapter 4 Survey Types (topographic, control, alignment, hydraulic)
- Tunnel survey with Tunnel Survey Manager requiring 10+ years experience in tunnel-specific surveying
- Deformation monitoring throughout tunnel boring operations
- Control network establishment for alignment verification
- Construction staking per MDOT Section 824 for all 70+ bridges
- As-built survey deliverables for all structures
- Hydrographic/hydraulic survey for drainage infrastructure

**Marketplace Robot/Sensor Relevance:**
- **Mobile LiDAR robot** for corridor mapping and topographic survey
- **Inspection drone** for 70+ bridge pre-construction condition documentation
- **Tunnel inspection robot** for deformation monitoring during boring
- **GPR robot** for pavement thickness and subsurface utility detection
- **Robotic total station** for continuous deformation monitoring

### 2. I-94 Drainage Tunnel Design-Build (Segment 3 Package 1)

**Agency:** MDOT
**Type:** Tunnel/Drainage infrastructure
**URL:** https://www.michigan.gov/mdot/business/contractors/innovativecontracting/i-94-drainage-tunnel
**RFQ Documents:**
- [RFQ](https://www.michigan.gov/mdot/-/media/Project/Websites/MDOT/Business/Contractors/Innovative-Contracting/I94-drainage-tunnel/RFQ.pdf)
- [Addendum 1](https://www.michigan.gov/mdot/-/media/Project/Websites/MDOT/Business/Contractors/Innovative-Contracting/I94-drainage-tunnel/RFQ-Addend1.pdf)
- [Q&A](https://www.michigan.gov/mdot/-/media/Project/Websites/MDOT/Business/Contractors/Innovative-Contracting/I94-drainage-tunnel/Q-A.pdf)
**Date:** RFQ issued October 2025; construction start late 2026/early 2027

**Project Scope:**
5,835-6,135 foot soft ground drainage tunnel, 16-17 foot inner diameter, with launch shaft, retrieval shaft, two offline drop shafts, junction chamber, pump station, adit tunnel connections.

**Survey Requirements (quoted from RFQ):**
- "Tunnel Survey Manager shall have 10 years' experience in the preparation, management, and performance of surveying and construction staking activities with at least five (5) years performing underground or tunnel surveying"
- "Tunnel Survey Manager shall demonstrate proficiency in tunnel-specific surveying techniques, including control network establishment, deformation monitoring, and tunnel alignment verification"
- Pre-construction topographic and utility survey
- Continuous alignment monitoring during TBM operations
- As-built documentation of all tunnel segments

**Marketplace Robot/Sensor Relevance:**
- **Tunnel inspection robot** with LiDAR for alignment verification and deformation monitoring
- **Robotic total station** for continuous tunnel alignment tracking
- **3D scanning robot** for as-built documentation of tunnel segments

### 3. M-14/I-96 Reconstruction (Sheldon Rd to Newburgh Rd)

**Agency:** MDOT (Great Lakes Engineering Group - consultant)
**Type:** Highway reconstruction with 17 bridge structures
**URL:** https://m14reconstruction.org/
**Value:** $130M (bridge portion alone)
**Date:** Construction 2025-2026 (westbound lanes in 2026)

**Project Scope:**
Reconstruction of M-14/I-96 with 17 structures requiring rehabilitation and reconstruction. Includes deck replacement, deck patching, epoxy overlays, substructure repairs, rocker realignments, structural steel repairs, crack injection, pin and hanger replacement.

**Survey Requirements:**
- Full MDOT design survey for 4.5-mile corridor
- Bridge-specific surveys for all 17 structures per MDOT Chapter 4 standards
- Construction staking per MDOT Section 824
- Cross-sections at 50-foot intervals
- Photogrammetric/LiDAR corridor mapping likely for design phase
- As-built surveys for all rehabilitated structures
- Hydraulic surveys for bridge waterway adequacy

**Marketplace Robot/Sensor Relevance:**
- **Mobile LiDAR vehicle** for corridor topographic mapping
- **Bridge inspection drone** for 17-structure condition documentation
- **Robotic total station** for construction staking automation
- **Under-bridge inspection robot** for substructure assessment

### 5. New Lock at the Soo (Sault Ste. Marie)

**Agency:** USACE Detroit District
**Type:** Navigation lock construction
**URL:** https://www.lrd.usace.army.mil/detroit/
**Value:** $2.62B (revised estimate)
**Date:** Construction ongoing through 2030; Phase 3 options awarded June 2025 ($95.3M)

**Project Scope:**
Construction of new 1,200 ft x 110 ft x 32 ft navigation lock in footprint of decommissioned Sabin and Davis locks. Includes blasting, bedrock excavation, concrete monolith placement, mechanical systems.

**Survey Requirements:**
- Hydrographic survey of St. Marys River channel
- Construction survey for massive concrete monolith placement
- Deformation monitoring throughout construction
- Bathymetric surveys for underwater work
- Precision alignment surveys for lock chamber walls (tolerances in mm)
- As-built documentation meeting USACE standards

**Marketplace Robot/Sensor Relevance:**
- **Marine survey robot (USV)** for hydrographic/bathymetric surveys
- **3D scanning robot** for massive concrete structure as-built documentation
- **Deformation monitoring sensors** (robotic total stations)
- **Underwater inspection ROV** for submerged structure verification

## Standard Specifications Referenced

These Michigan-specific survey standards appear across multiple projects and define the technical requirements that marketplace robots/sensors must meet.

### MDOT 2020 Standard Specifications for Construction

**URL:** https://www.michigan.gov/mdot/-/media/Project/Websites/MDOT/Business/Construction/Standard-Specifications-Construction/2020-Standard-Specifications-Construction.pdf
**Effective:** August 2025 lettings and later

**Key Survey Sections:**
- **Section 104.09 - Lines, Grades, and Elevations:** Core survey requirements for all MDOT construction projects
  - Horizontal control points maximum 1000 feet apart on tangent
  - Bench marks every 1000 feet along projects
  - Cross-sections every 50 feet for quantity determination
  - Hard surfaces to nearest 0.01 foot; ground to nearest 0.1 foot
  - Leveling loop checks within 0.01 foot

- **Section 824 - Construction Surveying and Staking:** Contractor staking requirements when included as pay item
  - Annual third-party equipment calibration required
  - Licensed professional surveyor oversight
  - Quality Control Plan submission required
  - Control verification before construction start

### MDOT Survey Standards of Practice (2014)

**URL:** https://mdotjboss.state.mi.us/SpecProv/getPubDoc.htm?docGuid=0d2aa85b-f99d-4c1a-9c03-0121f7d6a321&fileName=%22MDOT_Survey_Standards_2014.pdf%22

Comprehensive standards governing all survey work for MDOT.

### MDOT Design Manual - Chapter 4: Survey Types

**URL:** https://mdotwiki.state.mi.us/design/index.php/Chapter_4_-_Survey_-_Types

**Survey Types Defined:**
1. **Control Survey** - Primary/intermediate control establishment
2. **Alignment/Right of Way Survey** - Legal boundary retracement
3. **Design Survey (Topographic)** - Surface feature mapping for design
4. **Bridge/Structure Survey** - Structure location and integration
5. **Hydraulic Survey** - Data for bridge/culvert design
6. **Certified Survey** - Real estate acquisition documentation
7. **Photogrammetric Control Survey** - Ground control for aerial mapping

**Key Accuracy Standards (95% Confidence):**
| Control Type | Horizontal | Vertical |
|---|---|---|
| Primary Control | +/-0.05 ft | +/-0.05 ft closure |
| Intermediate Control | +/-0.07 ft avg; +/-0.10 ft max | +/-0.10 ft accumulated |
| Design Survey Hard Surfaces | +/-0.05 ft | +/-0.01 ft; +/-0.02 ft relative |
| Topographic Ground | N/A | +/-0.10 ft accumulated |

**Monument Requirements:**
- Primary: Metal disk in 5-foot deep concrete, below frost line
- Intermediate: #5 rebar 36 inches with survey cap
- Spacing: Freeway 650-1320 ft; Rural Arterial 350-900 ft; Urban Arterial 300 ft min

### MDOT Design Manual - Chapter 9: Remote Sensing

**URL:** https://mdotwiki.state.mi.us/design/index.php/Chapter_9_-_Remote_Sensing

**Static Terrestrial Laser Scanning (STLS) Standards:**
- Point density: max 0.1 ft spacing on structures, max 0.3 ft on road surfaces
- Minimum 4 control points per project
- Minimum 20% scan overlap
- Minimum 20 hard surface check observations
- Validation within 0.05 ft of adjusted point cloud
- Deliverables: e57, LAS, or LAZ format point clouds
- NSSDA-formatted accuracy reports required

**Mobile Terrestrial LiDAR (MTL) Standards:**
- Must meet NCHRP Report 748 Category 1A (High accuracy, Fine density) for most MDOT design projects
- Acquisition on dry pavement at traffic speeds
- RGB colorization images required along trajectory
- Local transformation mandatory
- One-third to two-thirds of control targets withheld for independent validation
- Check shots every 100 feet for linear projects >0.5 miles
- Validation within 0.05 ft of final adjusted point cloud
- Dual-frequency GNSS at 1 Hz or faster required
- IMU required; DMI suggested
- Deliverables: DTM, DGN, TIN, LAS, POD (colorized), photo mosaic

### MDOT Construction Manual - Section 104.09

**URL:** https://mdotwiki.state.mi.us/construction/index.php/104.09_Lines,_Grades,_and_Elevations

**Field Measurement Standards:**
- Horizontal distances: nearest 0.01 foot
- Angles at control points: nearest 5 seconds
- Rod/instrument distance: max 250 feet
- Bench mark loop tolerance: 0.03 foot after grading
- Clearing stake measurements: nearest 1 foot
- Cross-sections: max 25 feet between shots (left and right)

### MDOT Construction Manual - Section 824: Construction Surveying and Staking

**URL:** https://mdotwiki.state.mi.us/construction/index.php?title=824_-_Construction_Surveying_and_Staking

**Staking Types:**
- Clearing stakes (every 50 ft at right angles to centerline)
- Slope stakes (delineate cut/fill)
- Subgrade stakes (guide final grade elevation)
- Pavement stakes (edge and elevation alignment)
- Drainage and utility stakes (location and grade)
- ROW stakes (100 ft intervals, 50 ft on curves)

**Automated Machine Guidance (AMG):**
Supplemental procedures provided for federal/state funded projects using AMG, with dedicated verification forms.

### USGS LiDAR Base Specification (QL2)

Referenced by MiSAIL program:
- Quality Level 2
- 18.3 cm Fundamental Vertical Accuracy
- Used for statewide LiDAR collection

### FAA AC 150/5300-18B

Airport survey standard referenced by EPM-25-01:
- Obstruction Identification Surface (OIS) survey per FAR Part 77
- Airport GIS data collection standards
- Runway/navaid reference data survey

### AASHTO Standards Referenced in Michigan Contracts

- AASHTO survey accuracy standards for transportation projects
- NCHRP Report 748: Guidelines for Mobile LiDAR in Transportation Applications
- ASTM D5340: Standard Test Method for Airport Pavement Condition Index Surveys
- ASTM D6087: GPR for pavement evaluation
- NSSDA (National Standard for Spatial Data Accuracy) for reporting
