# VC Pitch Critique: YAK Robotics

**Reviewer:** Simulated seed-stage partner, construction-tech fund
**Date:** 2026-03-29
**Verdict:** Interesting thesis, not yet fundable. Would take a second meeting.

## What Makes Me Lean Forward

- **The problem slide is the best slide.** Nine steps, 10-15 days, phone-and-rolodex procurement -- that is a real, felt pain. The 43-RFP decomposition shows someone actually looked at MDOT lettings instead of Googling "construction market size." That is rare.
- **0/15 GCs have in-house drone survey.** If true, that is a genuine greenfield signal. Most founders claim greenfield; this one has a denominator.
- **12% buyer-side take rate with operator-gets-100%.** Smart positioning. Operators will onboard because there is zero cost to them. The fee is defensible given escrow + compliance + QA bundled in.
- **The "why now" slide has teeth.** Part 107 maturity + sub-$30K survey-grade LiDAR + MCP protocol convergence is a real timing argument, not hand-waving about AI.

## What Makes Me Skeptical

- **Zero customers, zero revenue, zero LOIs.** The traction slide is a dev metrics slide wearing a traction costume. 147 tests is not traction. A single signed LOI from one Michigan GC would be worth more than the entire codebase.
- **"0 direct competitors" on the cover slide is investor repellent.** It screams "I haven't looked" or "the market doesn't exist." The founder feedback already flagged this. The fact that it is still on the cover tells me nobody on the team has pitched to a skeptical audience yet.
- **Revenue in month 4 with no team, no customers, and no co-founders.** The deck is asking for $2.5M while simultaneously looking for a technical co-founder AND a BD co-founder. Who is building this in months 1-4? The solo founder?
- **The lunar slide.** I have seen 500 decks this year. Every robotics founder puts space on the roadmap. It does not make me excited; it makes me worry you are not focused. "v4.0 / 2028+" for lunar operations is not a plan, it is a fantasy that undermines your construction credibility.
- **$12-15M cap with no revenue and no team.** Comparable seed rounds listed (Built Robotics, DroneDeploy) had founding teams of 2-3 with industry backgrounds. This is a solo founder asking for a team-stage valuation at a pre-team stage.

## Where I Smell Bullshit vs. Genuine Insight

- **Bullshit:** "$8B TAM" with no citation. "58% cost reduction" with no source. "60-80% cost reduction" on the solution slide. These are round numbers that feel invented.
- **Bullshit:** "The intersection is empty." Fabric/OpenMind raised $20M from Pantera and is building robot economy infrastructure on Base. They are one product decision away from being a competitor.
- **Genuine:** The MDOT RFP analysis. The 9-step procurement breakdown. The specific equipment callout (DJI Matrice 350 RTK + Zenmuse L2 at $26K). The PLS stamp and LandXML deliverable format awareness. This founder has done real domain homework.
- **Genuine:** Buyer-side fee structure. Most marketplace founders default to seller-side fees and then wonder why supply won't onboard.

## Questions I Would Ask in the Meeting

1. Have you talked to a single GC? Not "identified" -- talked to. What did they say?
2. Why would a drone operator use your platform instead of their existing GC relationships?
3. What happens when DroneDeploy adds a bidding feature? They have 10K+ customers already.
4. Walk me through the unit economics of one real MDOT task end to end. What does the operator actually take home after fuel, insurance, processing software?
5. You are looking for two co-founders. Why should I fund you before you have found them? What have you done to recruit?
6. What is your unfair advantage that is not "first mover"? First mover in a market nobody is in is not an advantage.

## Single Biggest Weakness

**No demand-side validation whatsoever.** There is deep research, a working prototype, and serious domain knowledge -- but not one shred of evidence that a GC would actually change their behavior and buy surveys through a platform. Construction is a trust-based, relationship-driven industry. The entire thesis depends on GCs abandoning their existing survey firm relationships for a software platform run by someone they have never met. That behavioral change is the hardest part, and the deck does not address it at all.

## What Would Make Me Write a Check

One signed LOI from a Michigan GC for a paid pilot. One operator agreement. And a co-founder with a construction Rolodex. Give me those three things and I would lead the round at $10M cap.

## Proposed 2x2 Competitive Positioning Matrix

**X-axis: Procurement Automation** (Manual/phone-based ... AI-agent-mediated)
**Y-axis: Physical Execution** (Software-only/data layer ... Owns task completion with real robots in the field)

```
                        AI-Agent Mediated
                              |
     Fabric/OpenMind          |        YAK Robotics
     (protocol layer,         |        (AI procurement +
      no task execution)      |         physical delivery)
                              |
  Software ------------------- ------------------- Physical
  Only                        |                    Execution
                              |
     BuildingConnected        |        Traditional Survey
     DroneDeploy              |        Firms / Botshare
     (software tools,         |        (physical work,
      manual procurement)     |         phone procurement)
                              |
                        Manual/Phone
```

This is the right framing because it shows the white space is real: nobody automates procurement AND delivers physical execution. But it also honestly shows that the bottom-right quadrant (traditional firms) is who you are actually displacing, and they have relationships you do not.
